RPM Controller

Technology: Arduino Uno

This project was completed under the guidance of Professor Jason Goodman of Wheaton College, MA as part of his planetary physics research which sought to uncover more information about the geophysical processes of Jupiter's moon Europa. The purpose of the controller was to set and maintain the rpm of a motorized turnatable which would eventually hold a water tank that modeled the oceanic flow of europa. The main contsraints were that the controller had to accurately hit the targeted RPM without spilling any water, overshooting the targeted rpm, and it maintain the desired RPM consistently over time.

I first started by writing a simulator in the Arduino native language so that I could test out different controller algorithms. In the end, I used a PID controller algorithm similar to what is used on airplane autopilot systems. I then modeled the circuit on a breadboard to test, and eventually built to circuit board onto a layered Arduino Uno chip. Once final testing was complete, I designed a compact housing and mount which allowed users to easy interface with the system.
