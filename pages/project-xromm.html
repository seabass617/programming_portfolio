XROMM: Cane Toad Biomechanics

The purpose of this project was to assist professor Laura Ekstrom of Wheaton College, MA with her biomechanics research involving cane toad biomechancis.

X-Ray Reconstruction of Moving Morphology is a technique used in the biomechanics community to analyze animal morphology that consists of getting x-ray video of an animal in motion, reconstructing the bones structure & movement digitally, then analyzing the animation.

For this project I was tasked with reconstructing and cleaning bone models that come from CT scans of the cane toads. I used a combination of manual sculpting and poisson reconstruction to process the bone models and have them ready for animation. The next task was then do apply scientific rotoscoping to the bones models in order match the x-ray video jump. Once, this was complete, data was exported and further analyzed using Matlab.
